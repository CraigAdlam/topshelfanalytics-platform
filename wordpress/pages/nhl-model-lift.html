Player-level model lift compares each player’s completed 2+ shot baseline hit rate against the hit rate from model-qualified picks. Positive lift means the model performed better than the player’s raw baseline for that season.
How to read model lift

Each point represents one player-season for the 2+ shots threshold.

Baseline Hit Rate measures how often the player reached 2+ shots across all completed opportunities in that season.

Model Hit Rate measures how often the player reached 2+ shots only when they passed the model-quality threshold used in the publish pipeline.

Lift is the difference between model hit rate and baseline hit rate. For example, +8.0 percentage points means the model-qualified picks hit 8 points better than the player’s raw baseline.
